TaskLedger

Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how TaskLedger collects, uses, and stores information when you use the TaskLedger iOS application.

1. Information you create in the app

TaskLedger lets you create tasks, reminders, completion history, and related productivity data. This information is stored on your device. If iCloud sync is enabled for the app through Apple's services, that data may also sync through your Apple account.

2. Analytics and crash diagnostics

TaskLedger uses Firebase Analytics and Firebase Crashlytics, provided by Google, to understand how the app is used, improve features, diagnose crashes, and improve reliability.

Analytics events may include interactions such as task creation, task completion, navigation between screens, summary usage, reminder-related actions, and similar in-app product usage signals. Crash diagnostics may include device and app state information relevant to a failure.

TaskLedger does not use this analytics data for advertising.

3. Notifications

If you enable reminders, TaskLedger requests notification permission from iOS and schedules local notifications on your device. You can disable notifications at any time in the app or in your device settings.

4. How information is used

Information is used to:

  • operate the app and store your tasks and history,
  • show reminders you choose to enable,
  • measure product usage trends,
  • diagnose crashes and technical issues, and
  • improve the app's performance and user experience.

5. Sharing with third parties

TaskLedger uses third-party service providers to support analytics and crash reporting:

  • Google Firebase Analytics
  • Google Firebase Crashlytics
  • Apple services such as iCloud / CloudKit, when applicable

These providers process data according to their own terms and privacy documentation.

6. Data retention

Task data remains available until you delete it from the app. Diagnostic and analytics information retained by third-party services may be stored according to those providers' retention policies.

7. Your choices

  • You can delete tasks and task history from within the app.
  • You can disable notifications through iOS settings.
  • You can stop using the app at any time and remove it from your device.

8. Children's privacy

TaskLedger is not directed to children under 13, and the developer does not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.
